Code of Practice
Be Accountable to Affected Populations.
We allow our work to be guided by the humanitarian purpose and core principles, and are fiercely accountable to affected populations. this accountability recognizes the affected person as the primary source of knowledge, and primary user.
Open, Equitable, Collaborative.
We start by listening to alternative points of view, consider different approaches to measuring success and impact, and know how to be wrong in a responsible manner. Without open and equitable collaboration, the power of our community diminishes.
Will Not Make Weapons
We will not, under any circumstance, assist in the production of any items which serve the intention of inflicting harm on other people. We bear an onus of responsibility to work closely with users, which leads us to more personal, professional, and principled making.
Strive To Be Standard Bearers.
A standard bearer is a leader in the community who seeks to advance practice and processes in a responsible manner. We exemplify our respective professional standards and ethics, as well as those of the professional humanitarian community.
Challenge the System
There is a uniquely radical spirit within the humanitarian making community. We recognize this as a fundamental practice of the network - to challenge the system in ways that improve the efficiency and effectiveness of humanitarian response globally.
